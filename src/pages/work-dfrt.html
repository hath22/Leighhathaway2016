Defence Force Remuneration Tribunal

The Defence Force Remuneration Tribunal (DFRT) is an independent statutory authority that determines salary and relevant allowances for members of the Australian Defence Force.

The project was to provide a practical and easily operable website for members of the Defence Force to access relevant documents.

Design

I first became involved with this project at the visual design stage where I developed wireframes into polished designs in Adobe Illustrator.

As there wasn't an existing brand for the DFRT in place, I created branding options, representing equally the three defence sectors (Air Force, Navy, Army).

Below you can see a selection of the colour palettes I explored before presenting the first two options to the client.

The core templates comprising the majority of the site are the tables of documents and the content-heavy Inspections pages. The tables were designed with generous padding to enable users to easily navigate and digest the long tables of data. The Inspection pages utilised a narrow content area to ease the reading of potentially long blocks of text.

Development

Following the sign-off of the design, I undertook the HTML and CSS front-end development using SASS before handing over the CMS implementation to the development team.

The challenge during the development phase was to recreate the design whilst achieving WACG 2.0 AA accessibility, which led to some creative styling techniques.

Live site

The DFRT website went live in April 2016 and can be viewed at www.dfrt.gov.au
