Safer Together

Safer together is a government initiative to take a new approach to bushfire risk management under the newly formed Forest Fire Management Victoria.

The project was to provide a website for the Safer Together initiative that presented valuable information to a wide spectrum of users, from general public to industry experts.

My Involvement

I first became involved with this project at the visual design stage where I took completed wireframes and built up polished designs using Adobe Photoshop.

The final design deliverables included 6 different templates and 3 mobile responsive designs.

Following the sign-off of design I lead the front-end development and eventual implementantion of the site in Squiz Matrix.

Design

Safer Together although a new entity came with basic colour branding from it's parent government department but everything else was open to exploration.

To best display the map, chart and graph heavy content we opted for a modular system where a suite of predefined layouts allowed content authors to best display their data alongside supportive text.

In some sections of the site we knew there would be large amounts of text around a number of subsections, so to provide the best reading experience I opted for generous padding in and around each module giving greater focus to content.

The highest value content on the site are the charts and graphs so a large effort was put into making the charts accessible, easily digestable and aesthetically appealing.

Development

I cut the designs using SASS to produce final responsive HTML and CSS. The development was managed through Github with the back-end development team working on javascript and server side functionality.

Squiz Matrix doesn't accomodate modular authoring out of the box so during the implementation phase I pioneered a way to use the standard page metadata for authors to easily create and manage modules without web development skills.

Finally as part of the sitewide handover I wrote the site authoring guide and conducted Squiz Matrix training with the clients content authoring team.

Live site(s)

The Safer Together website went live in January 2016, and can be viewed at delwp.vic.gov.au/safer-together

Further to this we were engaged to create a new portal for the Forest Fire Management Victoria presence based on the template used for Safer Together. You can view this page at delwp.vic.gov.au/ffmvic
