Victorian Environmental Water holder

The Victorian Environmental Water Holder (VEWH) are an independent statutory body responsible for holding and managing Victoria's environmental water entitlements.

The project was to provide a completely new website for VEWH that met the goal of repurposing the site, from a tool for industry experts, to a digestible information source for the general public.

My Involvement

I first became involved with this project at the user experience stage in assisting the Experience manager with user workshops. As a part of this I created a few animated prototypes of interface interactions using After Effects to help present UI concepts to the client.

My primary responsibility was the visual design stage where I took completed wireframes and built up polished designs using Adobe Photoshop.

The final design deliverables included 8 different templates, 3 mobile responsive designs, a kitchen sink page with all site modules laid out for easy reference, and a pattern library of type styles, colours and various interaction states.

Following the sign-off of design I was heavily involved in the implementation of the site in Squiz Matrix, assiting the front-end development team in constructing the site templates.

Finally as part of the sitewide handover I wrote the site authoring guide and conducted Squiz Matrix training with the VEWH web team.

Design considerations

The VEWH had access to some fantastic imagery that was not being used on any digital medium. I utilised these to create high impact pages that help provide an engaging experience for public users which helps illustrate the benefits of environmental water.
